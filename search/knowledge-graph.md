# Knowledge Graph

Canonical source: [https://dokki.one/pub/docs/knowledge-graph](https://dokki.one/pub/docs/knowledge-graph)

Knowledge Graph helps an organization discover how people, companies, projects, topics, and resources are connected. It is useful when the answer depends on relationships rather than a single matching phrase.

## When to use it

- Find resources related to a customer, project, or concept.
- Understand how two people, organizations, or topics are connected.
- Improve search when several resources discuss the same subject in different words.

## Enable Knowledge Graph

Knowledge Graph is available for supported organization plans and is off until an organization owner or admin enables it. It works with organization workspaces; personal workspaces are not included.

## Ask relationship questions

Use Search, Copilot, or a supported connected client to ask questions such as “What is related to Project Atlas?” or “How is Company A connected to this launch?” Open the cited resources to confirm the relationship in its original context.

## Access and visibility

Being in an organization does not automatically give someone access to every workspace. Search results and resource links still follow workspace and resource permissions.

## Keep expectations realistic

- Connections improve as more relevant workspace content is added and updated.
- A suggested relationship is a discovery aid, not a replacement for checking the source.
- If a resource is missing, confirm that it is in an included organization workspace and that you can open it.
