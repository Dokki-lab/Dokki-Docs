Dokki organizes collaborative work around a few durable concepts: account context, workspace, resource, role, Copilot, agent, extension, message, calendar, and published site.

## Visual guide

[Home Workspace Map](home-workspace-map.md)

## Account context

Your account works in a Personal context or an organization context. The active context determines the available workspaces, agents, billing rules, AI controls, and organization policies.

## Workspace

A workspace is the shared operating space for resources, people, agents, messages, extensions, and publishing settings. It can be personal or organization-owned. Archived workspaces are removed from normal switching and MCP consent selection until restored.

## Resource

A resource is an item in the workspace tree: a document, table, artifact, file, folder, or agent. Resources can be organized, tagged, shared, or archived. Documents, tables, and artifacts can also be published as public pages.

## Role

Workspace roles set the baseline access. Direct resource roles refine access for one resource. Private resources remain hidden until the person has a direct role or an approved access request.

## Copilot

Copilot is the AI conversation surface. It can use the resources you may access, call eligible tools, and route longer work to agents. It does not bypass permissions, plan limits, organization policies, or external authorization.

## Agent

An agent is a persistent AI teammate with its own instructions, optional memory, skills, pinned context, MCP servers, workspace boundary, schedules, and run history.

## Extensions

Extensions add three workspace-level capabilities: Publish for public sites, Automation for code-backed routines, and Connectors for external AI clients or workspace-scoped MCP endpoints.

## Messages

Messages are real-time conversations between people, agents, and permitted MCP clients. Threads hold the replies and resulting work together.

## Calendar

Calendar is a read-only view of agent schedules, future occurrences, and completed runs. It does not synchronize with external calendar services.

## Published site

A published site is a public snapshot of selected workspace resources. Source edits remain private until you publish the new snapshot.
